Jute Fertilizer Recommendations

Proper fertilization is essential for healthy growth and optimal fiber yield in jute cultivation. Below are general guidelines for fertilizing jute:

Recommended Fertilizer Doses

  • **Nitrogen (N):** Apply 40 to 60 kg per hectare.
  • **Phosphorus (P2O5):** Apply 20 to 30 kg per hectare.
  • **Potassium (K2O):** Apply 20 to 30 kg per hectare.

These recommendations are based on the species of jute being cultivated. For *Corchorus olitorius*, a common recommendation is 40 kg N, 20 kg P2O5, and 20 kg K2O per hectare. For *Corchorus capsularis*, the recommendation is 60 kg N, 30 kg P2O5, and 30 kg K2O per hectare.

Application Timing

  • **Basal Application:** Incorporate the full dose of phosphorus and potassium, along with half of the nitrogen, into the soil during the final land preparation before sowing.
  • **Top Dressing:** Apply the remaining half of the nitrogen as a top dressing approximately 45 days after sowing.

Application Tips

  • Ensure even distribution of fertilizers to avoid nutrient imbalances.
  • Maintain proper soil moisture levels to facilitate nutrient uptake.
  • Conduct regular soil tests to tailor fertilization practices to specific soil conditions.

Note: Soil fertility and nutrient requirements can vary based on local conditions. It's advisable to conduct a soil test for precise recommendations tailored to your specific situation.
