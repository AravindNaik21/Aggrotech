Cotton Fertilizer Recommendations

Proper fertilization is essential for healthy growth and optimal yield in cotton cultivation. Below are general guidelines for fertilizing cotton:

Nitrogen (N)

  • **Application Rate:** Apply approximately 50 pounds of nitrogen per expected bale of cotton per acre. For instance, if the yield goal is two bales per acre, apply 100 pounds of nitrogen per acre.
  • **Timing:** Split the nitrogen application, applying one-third pre-plant and the remaining two-thirds at early bloom to meet the crop's peak demand during fruiting stages.

Phosphorus (P) and Potassium (K)

  • **Soil Testing:** Conduct soil tests to determine phosphorus and potassium levels. Apply fertilizers based on soil test recommendations to avoid deficiencies or excesses. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • **Application:** Incorporate phosphorus and potassium fertilizers into the soil before planting to ensure availability during early growth stages.

Soil pH Management

  • **Optimal pH:** Maintain soil pH between 6.0 and 6.5 for optimal nutrient availability.
  • **Liming:** If soil pH is below the optimal range, apply lime based on soil test recommendations to raise pH to the desired level.

Micronutrients

  • **Boron:** Consider annual boron applications, either to the soil or as foliar sprays, especially in boron-deficient soils, to support reproductive development.

Application Tips

  • **Even Distribution:** Ensure fertilizers are evenly distributed to prevent nutrient imbalances.
  • **Irrigation:** Incorporate fertilizers into the soil through irrigation or rainfall to enhance nutrient uptake.
  • **Monitoring:** Regularly monitor plant growth and soil nutrient levels, adjusting fertilization practices as needed.

Note: These recommendations are general guidelines. It's advisable to conduct regular soil tests and consult with local agricultural extension services for precise recommendations tailored to your specific conditions.
