Coconut Tree Fertilizer Recommendations

Proper nutrient management is essential for the healthy growth and productivity of coconut trees. Below are the recommended fertilizer practices:

Organic Manure

From the 5th year onwards, apply 50 kg of organic manure per palm annually. This can include farmyard manure (FYM), compost, or green manure. Organic manure improves soil structure and fertility.

Chemical Fertilizers

Apply the following chemical fertilizers per palm per year, split into two equal doses during June–July and December–January:

  • Urea: 1.3 kg (providing 560 g of Nitrogen)
  • Super Phosphate: 2.0 kg (providing 320 g of Phosphorus)
  • Muriate of Potash: 2.0 kg (providing 1,200 g of Potassium)

These fertilizers should be applied in circular basins of 1.8 meters radius from the base of the palm, incorporated into the soil, and followed by irrigation. Ensure sufficient soil moisture is present at the time of application.

Micronutrient Application

To address potential micronutrient deficiencies, apply the following per palm per year:

  • Borax: 50 g (to supply Boron)
  • Gypsum: 1.0 kg (to supply Calcium and Sulfur)
  • Magnesium Sulphate: 500 g (to supply Magnesium)

These should be applied along with the primary fertilizers in the same basins.

Bio-fertilizers

Incorporate bio-fertilizers to enhance nutrient availability:

  • Azospirillum: 50 g
  • Phosphobacteria: 50 g
  • Alternatively, Azophos: 100 g

Mix these bio-fertilizers with compost or FYM and apply near the feeding roots once every six months, starting from planting. Avoid mixing with chemical fertilizers and pesticides.

Organic Recycling

Utilize green manure crops and organic waste to enrich the soil:

  • Sow green manure crops like sunnhemp, wild indigo, calapogonium, or daincha, and plough them in situ at flowering as a substitute for compost.
  • Sow sunnhemp at 50 g per palm in the basin and incorporate before flowering.
  • Apply coir pith compost, vermicompost made from coir pith, coconut leaves, or other wastes from the coconut grove.

Special Considerations

For mite resistance, increase the application of Muriate of Potash to 3.5 kg per palm per year. Additionally, neem cake can be applied at 5 kg per palm per year to improve soil health and provide pest resistance.

Regular soil and leaf analysis can help tailor the fertilization program to specific nutrient needs and optimize coconut production.
