Mango Fertilizer Recommendations

Proper fertilization is essential for healthy growth and optimal fruit production in mango trees. Below are general guidelines for fertilizing mango trees:

Fertilization Schedule

  • Young Trees (1-3 Years Old): Apply 200-300 grams of a complete fertilizer per tree annually, split into two equal doses. The first application should be at the start of the rainy season, and the second just before the end of the rainy season. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}
  • Mature Trees (4 Years and Older): Apply 1.5-2.5 kg of a complete fertilizer per tree annually, split into two equal doses as described above.

Fertilizer Types

  • Complete Fertilizers: Use balanced formulations that provide essential nutrients, such as nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K).
  • Foliar Sprays: During wet months (July to November), apply a 2-2.5% potassium nitrate (KNO₃) solution. During dry months (December to May), use a 1-1.5% KNO₃ solution.

Application Tips

  • Distribute fertilizer evenly under the canopy of the tree, maintaining a distance of 45-90 cm from the trunk. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • Incorporate the fertilizer into the soil by digging holes or a shallow canal around the base of the tree, approximately 7.6-13 cm deep, following the tree canopy.
  • Water thoroughly after each application to help nutrients reach the root zone.
  • Monitor tree health and adjust fertilization based on growth, leaf color, and soil test results.

Note: Soil conditions and nutrient needs can vary. It's advisable to conduct a soil test for precise recommendations tailored to your specific conditions.
