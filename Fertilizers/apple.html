Fertilizer Recommendations for Apple Trees

Proper fertilization is essential for the health and productivity of apple trees. Below are guidelines to help you manage nutrient levels effectively:

Nitrogen (N)

Annual shoot growth should be around 6-10 inches per year. If growth is outside this range, adjust nitrogen rates accordingly. A general recommendation is to apply 0.1 lbs of nitrogen per year of tree age, up to a maximum of 0.5-0.8 lbs.

Phosphorus (P) and Potassium (K)

Phosphorus and potassium should be applied before planting based on soil test recommendations. After the trees mature, fertilization with phosphorus and potassium may not be required; however, periodic soil testing should be done to monitor levels of these elements and soil pH.

Soil pH

Maintain soil pH in the range of 6.0 for the subsoil to 6.5 for the topsoil, as pH influences the availability of various nutrients to the plant.
